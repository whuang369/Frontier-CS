Let's call a set of positive integers $S$ **correct** if the following two conditions are met:

- $S \subseteq \{1, 2, \dots, n\}$;
- If $a \in S$ and $b \in S$, then $|a-b| \ne x$ and $|a-b| \ne y$.

For the given values $n$, $x$, and $y$, you have to find the maximum size of a correct set.

## Input format
- A single line contains three integers $n$, $x$ and $y$ ($1 \le n \le 10^9$; $1 \le x, y \le 2^{22}$).

## Output format
- Print one integer â€” the maximum size of a correct set.

## Scoring 
- Assume the ground truth answer is $ans$, and your answer is $cnt$.
- Your score is $max(0, 1 - \log_{10}(abs(cnt - ans) + 1) / 10)$.
